Quality research and analysis in Sierra Leone

Frontier Research Ltd is a private equity-backed research firm based in Sierra Leone. Founded in 2015 to meet the demand for high quality data and analysis, we serve a broad client base comprised of governments, multilateral institutions, NGOs, foundations and multinationals.

About us

Frontier Research’s multidisciplinary team is drawn from a diverse range of professions, including international development, risk management and private equity. What unites us is a long-standing interest in West Africa – and a passion for producing quality research.

Since its inception in 2015, Frontier Research has sought to bridge the information gap that exists in Sierra Leone and Liberia, countries where available data often is incomplete, out of date, unreliable or unpublished. In the process, we have executed numerous projects for a range of clients, including the World Bank, the International Finance Corporation (IFC), GIZ, UNDP and the Soros Economic Development Fund (SEDF).

Services

1

CSR and Community Engagement Services

From designing survey instruments to analysing data collected in the field, our team has extensive experience conducting quantitative and qualitative research in Sierra Leone and Liberia.

Throughout the recruitment and training of enumerators, survey piloting and management of field deployments, we incorporate strict quality control mechanisms in our research process to ensure the data we collect is trustworthy. We collect data using tablets, which allows our core team to review the data that has been uploaded at the end of each day, and randomly check-in on our field researchers.

2

Technical Assistance & Analysis

Our approach to technical assistance is informed by our understanding of the local political economy.

Overcoming obstacles to effective reform or realising development objectives isn’t simply a matter of adequate financing and technical expertise. Appreciating the inter-play between politics and local economic dynamics is paramount to providing effective technical assistance. Our team’s ability to combine their thematic expertise with local knowledge is at the core of the services we provide.

Governance

  • Transparency and accountability in the extractives sector
  • Supporting the EITI process in West Africa
  • Stakeholder engagement in the mining sector
  • Political economy analysis
  • Risk analysis

Private sector development

  • SME capacity development in fragile states
  • Impact investing in conflict-affected and fragile states
  • Market analysis
  • Business reform in conflict-affected and fragile states

Public health

  • Health systems strengthening
  • Health financing
  • Management and delivery of health services
  • Performance based financing in the health sector
3

CSR and Community Engagement Services

Too often, the activities of well-intentioned agriculture and extractives companies are disrupted in Africa because relations with host communities have broken down.

Relationship break-downs of this nature have commercial consequences – and can carry significant reputational risk. Our team has successfully supported multinationals, helping them understand the local dynamics and communicate more effectively with stakeholders.

Our team

Lyttelton BraimaManaging Director

Carole is a global health Consultant with 28 years of expertise in health and social care policy, planning and service delivery in the UK and internationally. She currently serves as a Director at Frontier Research and is the Director of Manchester Global Foundation, a charitable trading organisation she established in 2015. Carole has eight years’ experience working in the government, NGO and private sectors across sub-Saharan Africa, including three years living in Sierra Leone. While in Sierra Leone, Carole worked as a senior technical and management adviser for a year in the Ministry of Health and Sanitation. Before moving to Sierra Leone, Carole worked in the UK’s National Health Service (NHS) for over 20 years, where she fulfilled a number of project management and senior leadership roles across the organisation.

Carole holds a Postgraduate Diploma in Public Health from the University of Liverpool and an MA in Engineering Sciences from the University of Oxford.
